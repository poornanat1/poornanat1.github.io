About Me

I'm a Senior Machine Learning Engineer at Latham & Watkins, where I design AI systems for complex legal work. I fine‑tune open‑source language models and build retrieval‑augmented generation platforms with a focus on transparency, accountability, and reliability.

My path began in the White House mailroom, selecting letters for President Obama. Since then, I’ve led pandemic data reporting for federal agencies, built semantic search tools for military policy, and deployed AI in high‑stakes environments. Across each role, I’ve seen that technology is only as effective as the context and governance behind it.

Good AI is not just accurate—it is accountable. Judgment, interpretation, and governance remain fundamentally human, and great systems make space for them.

At Palantir, I helped reduce job‑classification timelines from months to minutes and built search pipelines that brought order to ambiguous policy. Earlier, at Deloitte, I led COVID‑19 reporting for federal leadership and learned first‑hand how fragile data pipelines can shape public outcomes.

Today, my work lives at the intersection of machine learning and public impact: building systems that are rigorous and scalable while embedding safeguards that make them equitable and trustworthy.
